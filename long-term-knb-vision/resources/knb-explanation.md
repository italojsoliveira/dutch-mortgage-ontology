The **Koninklijke Notariële Beroepsorganisatie (KNB)** is the statutory professional body for civil-law notaries in the Netherlands. It sets professional standards, regulates practice, supports members, and organizes knowledge and digital infrastructure used across the profession.

## How KNB structures notarial practice

To organize guidance, education, model deeds, and digital workflows, KNB groups notarial work into **practice domains (“domeinen”)**.  
Each domain clusters **types of deeds** that share legal subject matter and regulatory frameworks.

## Main KNB domains and the deed types they cover

### 1. Real Estate Law (Onroerend Goed / Registergoed)

Focus: rights in immovable property and land registration.  
**Typical deed types**

- Transfer of ownership (sale and purchase deeds)
    
- Mortgage deeds
    
- Deeds of division (apartment rights / partition)
    
- Ground lease (erfpacht) and superficies
    
- Establishment or amendment of easements
    
- Property development and project deeds
    

### 2. Family and Personal Law (Personen- en Familierecht)

Focus: legal relationships between individuals and estate planning.  
**Typical deed types**

- Wills and codicils
    
- Prenuptial and postnuptial agreements
    
- Cohabitation agreements
    
- Estate settlement and inheritance declarations
    
- Gifts and donation deeds
    
- Living wills / powers of attorney (estate planning instruments)
    

### 3. Corporate Law (Ondernemingsrecht)

Focus: legal entities, companies, and governance.  
**Typical deed types**

- Incorporation of companies (e.g., BV, NV, foundations, associations)
    
- Amendments to articles of association
    
- Share transfers
    
- Legal mergers and demergers
    
- Issuance of shares or depositary receipts
    
- Corporate restructurings and conversions

# How KNB domains function operationally

The domains are not just conceptual groupings. They are embedded in the **professional ecosystem** of Dutch notaries and structure:

1. Knowledge and guidance
    
2. Model deed libraries

---

## 1. Knowledge & policy structure

Each domain has its own:

- **Professional committees** (vakcommissies)
    
- Practice notes and guidance (handreikingen)
    
- Regulatory interpretations
    
- Risk and compliance frameworks
    

For example:

- Real estate guidance focuses heavily on land registry interaction and AML risks in property transactions.
    
- Family law guidance addresses capacity, undue influence, and succession planning.
    
- Corporate law focuses on governance, UBO registration, and transaction structuring.

## 2. Model deed libraries (Modellen)

KNB maintains extensive **standard model deeds** used across the profession.  
These are organized strictly by domain because legal logic and mandatory clauses differ.

### Domain → typical model sets

**Real Estate**

- Transfer deed models (residential, commercial, new build)
    
- Mortgage templates
    
- Easement clauses
    
- Apartment rights division
    

**Family Law**

- Modular will structures (single, mirror, complex estate planning)
    
- Marital property regimes
    
- Gift structures with conditions
    
- Powers of attorney
    

**Corporate Law**

- BV incorporation packages
    
- Shareholders’ resolutions
    
- Merger/de-merger templates
    
- Foundation governance clauses
    

👉 In practice, notarial software often mirrors this domain structure, so selecting a domain determines which clause libraries are available.
